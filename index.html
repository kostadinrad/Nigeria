Nigeria iGaming Strategic Report – March 2026

Following the November 2024 Supreme Court ruling that confined the National Lottery Act to the Federal Capital Territory, Nigeria has moved decisively to a state‑led model coordinated by the Federation of State Gaming Regulators of Nigeria (FSGRN). The country now hosts one of Africa’s largest gambling markets, supported by a young, mobile‑first population and recent fiscal reforms that standardise gaming taxes and licence fees across states.

Nigeria’s gambling industry is projected to generate about $3.63 billion by end‑2025, with over 60 million Nigerians placing bets and online gambling turnover estimated at ₦5.6 trillion, underscoring both scale and regulatory importance.

1. Market Landscape & Leading Brands

Sports betting is the dominant form of gambling in Nigeria, accounting for the majority of the sector’s revenue. This is driven by deep-rooted football fandom and a smartphone-enabled population. The leading operators include Bet9ja, BetKing, and SportyBet, who maintain high brand visibility through extensive local retail networks and sports sponsorships.

  • Target demographic: Core players are 18–40‑year‑old males in urban centers like Lagos and Abuja.
  • Device usage: 90% of online activity is mobile-first, utilizing "Lite" versions of platforms to minimize data costs.

2. Regulatory Hierarchy & Licensing Paths

2.1 State Regulators & FSGRN

The Supreme Court decision clarified that states hold exclusive power over games of chance in their territories. To prevent fragmentation, states have coordinated through the Federation of State Gaming Regulators of Nigeria (FSGRN). This body has introduced a unified 11% tax on Gross Gaming Revenue (GGR) and standardized annual licence fees of ₦100 million per category.

2.2 NLRC Federal Role & Remote Permits

The National Lottery Regulatory Commission (NLRC) remains the authority for the FCT (Abuja). While it still issues Remote Operator Permits ($100,000/year), these permits no longer grant operational rights within states like Lagos or Oyo without a separate state license.

Strategic Insight: Operators must treat the FCT as a standalone jurisdiction while pursuing FSGRN-standardized licenses for broader national coverage.

4. Advertising Constraints (2025/2026)

A major structural change for the 2026 market is the Google ban on gambling advertising targeting Nigeria, which went into effect January 8, 2025. This prohibits the promotion of any gambling products across Search, YouTube, and the Display Network.

  • Alternative Channels: Operators have pivoted to influencer marketing, high-impact SMS campaigns, and local affiliate networks.
  • ARCON Vetting: All physical and digital advertising must be pre-approved by the Advertising Regulatory Council of Nigeria (ARCON), which maintains strict rules on timing and celebrity endorsements.
Marketing Note: The loss of Google Search as an acquisition channel has significantly increased the Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), favoring operators with established brand loyalty or retail "street" presence.
