Nigeria iGaming Strategic Report – March 2026

Following the November 2024 Supreme Court ruling, Nigeria has shifted to a State-Led "Universal Reciprocity" model. The market is currently Africa’s largest, defined by a massive youth demographic and a significant fiscal overhaul under the Nigerian Tax Act (NTA) 2025.

1. Market Landscape & Leading Brands

The Nigerian market is highly consolidated among top-tier operators. Bet9ja remains the market leader with approximately 35% market share, followed by BetKing and SportyBet.

  • Target Demographic: 60 million active punters (ages 18-40).
  • Device Usage: 90% of bets are mobile-first, utilizing affordable Android devices and lite-mobile versions of platforms.

2. Regulatory Hierarchy & Licensing Paths

2.1 The FSGRN State License

The Federation of State Gaming Regulators (FSGRN) now manages a harmonized regime across 24+ states. The "Universal License" allows operators to launch across member states with standardized fees.

2.2 The NLRC Federal Remote Permit

For offshore operators, the NLRC still offers a Remote Operator Permit (ROP) at $100,000 USD/annum. However, this permit is no longer recognized in major states like Lagos, posing significant legal risks without local state licensing.

4. The "Triple-Dip" Fiscal Regime (2026)

Profitability models must account for a consolidated tax burden that includes:

  • State GGR Tax: 11% (FSGRN standardized).
  • Federal Excise Duty: 5% on all gaming services (introduced Jan 2025).
  • Withholding Tax (WHT): 5% auto-deducted from player winnings.
  • Corporate Income Tax (CIT): Up to 30% on net profits under NTA 2025.

5. Compliance & Infrastructure

5.1 Real-Time Monitoring (Sentinel)

Operators must integrate with state monitoring systems like Sentinel or state-specific API dashboards for real-time visibility of stakes and automated tax reporting. Manual logs are no longer accepted.

5.2 Payments & Marketing

  • Fintech Integration: Mandatory support for Paystack and Flutterwave (1.5%–2% fees).
  • The Ad Ban: Google has restricted gambling ads in Nigeria following the Supreme Court decentralization. Marketing must pivot to local influencers, SMS, and physical retail presence.
